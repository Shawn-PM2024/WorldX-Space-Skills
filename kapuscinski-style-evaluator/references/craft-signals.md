# Craft Signals

Use this file when the main `SKILL.md` needs more texture but should stay compact.

This is not a theory essay. Treat it as a signal bank for commentary.

## 1. Observation Layer

### Stronger signals

- The prose begins from something seen, heard, physically arranged, or socially positioned.
- Detail does not merely decorate atmosphere; it changes the reader's understanding.
- The writer lets perception arrive before explanation.
- Scene material introduces pressure, asymmetry, ritual, awkwardness, or contradiction without naming it too early.

### Weaker signals

- The paragraph begins with interpretation, and the scene arrives only as proof.
- Detail accumulates, but each item is interchangeable with the next.
- The text wants symbolic force without first earning observational credibility.
- The writer seems to know what the scene means before the scene has had time to appear.

### Commentary cues

- "The scene is present, but it still functions more as illustration than as discovery."
- "This detail matters because it changes the social pressure in the paragraph."
- "You are naming the meaning before the material has had time to generate it."

## 2. Narration Layer

### Stronger signals

- The narrator is present, but not theatrically central.
- The text gives the subject enough room to appear before being finalized by commentary.
- Movement comes from sequence, juxtaposition, pacing, and tonal restraint.
- The paragraph trusts pressure, arrangement, or implication more than repeated explanation.

### Weaker signals

- The narrator over-announces their intelligence, judgment, or moral position.
- Each paragraph tells the reader what to think before the paragraph has fully unfolded.
- Transition depends on summary or thesis restatement rather than textual motion.
- The writer substitutes insistence for pressure.

### Commentary cues

- "The narrator is visible here, but the subject has not yet been allowed full presence."
- "This transition explains too much; it does not yet grow from what the prose has already established."
- "The judgment is not wrong, but it arrives too early to be fully persuasive on the page."

## 3. Meaning-Generation Layer

### Stronger signals

- Larger significance emerges from local arrangement, not just from a concluding sentence.
- Reflection feels earned because the prose has already built enough ground.
- The passage widens from the concrete without losing contact with the concrete.
- Meaning grows by accumulation, contrast, relation, or delayed recognition.

### Weaker signals

- The writing reaches for civilization, history, or fate before the scene can hold that weight.
- Reflection is present, but it feels imported rather than generated.
- The text is trying to be large before it has been exact.
- Elevation is achieved by rhetoric instead of by textual pressure.

### Commentary cues

- "The paragraph wants a larger horizon, but the local material is not yet carrying enough weight."
- "The reflective turn works because it grows from what has already been observed."
- "The passage is reaching upward, but it has not yet built enough pressure below."

## 4. Secondary Layers

Use these only when the draft itself gives you real grounds:

- historical awareness
- power asymmetry
- center-margin relations
- ethical pressure beneath description

Do not force these onto a local or modest assignment.

## 5. Misreadings To Avoid

- Do not mistake geopolitical subject matter for stylistic closeness.
- Do not mistake abstract seriousness for historical depth.
- Do not mistake lyrical diction for earned significance.
- Do not mistake symbolic ambition for observational weight.
