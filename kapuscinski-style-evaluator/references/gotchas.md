# Gotchas

Use this file when a request risks pushing the evaluator into a known failure mode.

## 1. Topic Matching Is Not Style Evaluation

Do not treat serious subject matter as stylistic closeness.

Common false positives:

- empire, dictatorship, war, poverty, borders, travel, Africa, revolution, or political collapse
- historical vocabulary without observational pressure
- moral seriousness without scene work

Better response:

- acknowledge the subject matter
- explain that the craft question is still observation, narration, and meaning-generation
- identify whether the page actually lets details carry weight

## 2. Do Not Expose The Diagnostic Framework Too Rigidly

The three diagnostic questions are for thinking, not default formatting.

Avoid responses that read like:

- `Observation Layer: ...`
- `Narration Layer: ...`
- `Meaning-Generation Layer: ...`

Better response:

- write like an editor
- begin with the strongest craft judgment
- weave the diagnostic logic into natural prose

## 3. No Scores Or Ranking Language

This skill should not output percentages, points, star ratings, or ranked closeness.

Avoid:

- "8/10 Kapuscinski-like"
- "70% close"
- "high score on observation, low score on meaning"

Better response:

- "This moves closer to the target manner in its patient scene entry, but remains distant in how quickly it explains the meaning."

## 4. Micro-Examples Must Stay Tiny

Micro-examples are allowed only to make a craft move tangible.

Do not:

- rewrite the whole passage
- continue for a full paragraph
- claim the example is authentic Kapuscinski
- perform dense signature mimicry

Better response:

- one to three sentences
- explicitly framed as an illustration
- focused on a move such as delaying judgment, letting detail carry pressure, or shifting from assertion to observation

## 5. Do Not Punish Modest Assignments For Lacking Historical Scope

Historical or civilizational lift is partly determined by subject matter.

If the draft is local, personal, or modest in scale, do not fault it simply for lacking empire-level scope.

Better response:

- focus on portable craft
- ask whether the prose observes, stages, and turns well
- use history or power only when the text provides real material

## 6. Avoid Literary Lectures Detached From The Draft

The user is asking for coaching on their text, not a miniature course.

Do not let references to `The Emperor`, `Shah of Shahs`, or `Travels with Herodotus` overtake the commentary.

Better response:

- mention a representative work only when it clarifies the craft point
- return quickly to the user's sentences, scene choices, and revision moves
